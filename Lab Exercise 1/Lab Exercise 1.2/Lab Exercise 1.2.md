This circuit uses an Arduino Uno to control a group of LEDs arranged in sequence on the breadboard. The Arduino is programmed to create a unique lighting pattern where the LEDs light up at the same time moving inward from both sides toward the center. As the outer LEDs turn on together, the pattern continues until the middle LED remains illuminated, making the center the focus of the sequence. After reaching the center, the lighting pattern changes direction, and the LEDs turn on simultaneously moving outward from the center toward both ends of the arrangement.

This creates a smooth and symmetrical running-light effect that looks visually appealing and demonstrates how the Arduino can control multiple outputs with timing and programmed sequences. Each LED is connected with a resistor to limit the flow of current and prevent damage to the components. The breadboard provides organized connections, while the Arduino sends digital signals that determine when each LED should switch on or off. This project is commonly used in learning basic electronics and programming because it helps demonstrate sequencing, synchronization, and control of multiple LEDs using code.
